# Human review norms

Objective checks establish that specified observations occurred; they do not establish that the
paper is correct or that the submission teaches the right lesson. Review the sealed candidate
report, reproduced artifacts, verifier evidence, source, and capsule-specific guide together.

Ask whether the implementation captures the claimed mechanism, whether controls rule out obvious
alternatives, whether scale reduction or proxy transfer is defensible, and whether the reported
limitations match the evidence. Look for hard-coded outputs, dependence on visible examples,
unstated deviations, brittle behavior, misleading visuals, or a verifier that rewards a proxy
without testing its transfer argument. Prefer compact counterexamples over line-by-line code review.

Qualitative conclusions remain prose. Do not turn this review into a hidden numeric model-judge
score. It is acceptable to ask another coding assistant for a review, using this file and the
capsule guide as its rubric, but the human owns the conclusion.
